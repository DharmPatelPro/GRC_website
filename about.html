About GTU Robotics Club (GRC)

GTU Robotics Club (GRC) is a vibrant and interdisciplinary community of innovation-driven students under Gujarat Technological University. GRC unites passionate minds from diverse domains—software, hardware, mechanical, and management—to explore, build, and compete in the ever-evolving world of robotics and automation.

Who We Are

Founded with the vision of promoting practical, hands-on learning, GRC serves as a bridge between academic knowledge and real-world application. Our goal is to equip students with future-ready skills through teamwork, mentorship, and exposure to the latest technologies.

  • A legacy being passed since 2016
  • 400+ trained students across batches contributing to national robotics innovation
  • Alumni working at top tech companies, research labs, and startups
  • Invited talks by industry professionals and professors to align student projects with the latest industry trends
  • Strong culture of documentation, code sharing (Git), and open-source practices

We believe in "learning by doing". Every member at GRC actively contributes to building robots from scratch—concept, design, development, programming, testing, and competition execution.

Vision

Our vision is to cultivate a community where curiosity leads to creation, and knowledge is shared through collaboration. We aim to be a hub of continuous learning, where students push boundaries in robotics through hands-on innovation, mentorship, and a passion for advancing technology.

Mission

"Our mission is to engage students in practical robotics challenges that cultivate technical expertise, foster innovation, and strengthen their understanding of core engineering principles through collaborative, hands-on experience."

How GRC Works

  • Annual Selection Process: Structured interview and task-based shortlisting.
  • Team Distribution: Members join software, hardware, mechanical, or management sub-teams.
  • Project-Based Learning: Weekly targets and monthly milestones to build competition-ready robots.
  • Knowledge Sharing: Regular training, peer mentoring, and senior talks.
  • Mini-Robocon: Members build mini-robots before entering full-scale competitions.
  • Mentorship-based hierarchy for smooth knowledge transition every year.

Theme Example (Robocon 2025)

Each year, the club works around a central theme defined by competitions like DD Robocon. The 2025 theme revolves around a Basketball Challenge, inspired by Mongolian traditions and sports. Robots are designed to dribble, pass, and shoot balls into a hoop using fully autonomous and manually controlled systems. The challenge tests speed, precision, coordination, and strategy.

Competitions We Participate In

  • ABU Robocon (DD Robocon India): Prestigious national/international robotics competition broadcast on Doordarshan. Teams design robots for game-based, time-bound tasks.
    Skills: Mechatronics, vision, wireless control, real-time systems.
  • e-Yantra (IIT Bombay): Project-based learning competition focused on solving real-world problems using robotics and embedded systems.
    Skills: Embedded C, ROS, Firebird V, problem-solving, teamwork.
  • Mitsubishi Electric Cup: National-level automation competition for factory automation and smart manufacturing solutions.
    Skills: PLC, SCADA, HMI, smart factory design, automation logic.
  • Hackathons & Innovation Challenges: Tech-fests, hackathons, and innovation challenges at IITs, NITs, and other universities.

Our Team

  • Software Team: Programming, algorithms, vision, AI, control logic.
  • Hardware Team: Circuit design, PCB, wiring, sensors, testing.
  • Mechanical Team: Frame, drive, actuators, CAD/CAM, 3D printing.
  • Management Team: Docs, sponsorship, PR, social media, logistics.

Each year, new students join the club and contribute towards building and evolving high-performing robots.

How to Join

  • Watch for recruitment notification on Instagram or college notice board.
  • Fill the application form (mention your area of interest).
  • Participate in the interview/task round.
  • Team allotment based on performance.
  • Induction and training for beginners before core projects.

Eligibility: Students from specific branches and years at GTU or its affiliated colleges.
